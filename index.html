Design For The Public 2024

A two-day event exploring public interest design, hosted in Portland, OR

📋 About the Event

Design For The Public 2024 was a community-led gathering for designers, technologists, public servants, and researchers working at the intersection of design and the public good.

This site serves as an archive of the event agenda, guides, and resources shared during the conference.

📚 Book List

  • The Color of Law by Richard Rothstein
  • Dark Matter and Trojan Horses by Dan Hill
  • What Design Can’t Do by Silvio Lorusso
  • Humble Inquiry by Edgar H. Schein
  • Radical Help by Hilary Cottam
  • Farther, Faster and Less Drama by Janice & Jason Fraser
  • The Mushroom at the End of the World by Anna Lowenhaupt Tsing
  • Let's Become Fungal by Yasmine Ostendorf-Rodríguez

🗓️ Conference Agenda

October 16 – Revolution Hall

  • Opening Keynote: Ron Bronson – “Designing the Moment”
  • Talk: Christian Crumlish – “Bridging Design & Product Management”
  • Panel: Career Paths in Public Interest Design
  • Talk: Dr. Shelly Ronen – “Tackling Child Welfare Challenges”

October 17 – Wacom Experience Center

  • Panel: “Breaking Silos” – Collaboration with Stakeholders
  • Show & Tell / Crit Sessions
  • Workshop: Trauma-Informed Design for Public Services
